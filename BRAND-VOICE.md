# Brand Voice Guidelines
## Spotless Carwash — Forest Park, IL
### Analysis Date: 2026-05-08

---

## Voice Summary

Spotless Carwash speaks like a confident neighborhood operator who's been on the corner for 30 years and has nothing to prove — direct, plainspoken, and lightly playful, with a strong bias for short declarative sentences and concrete numbers (4½ minutes, $4 / 5 minutes, 7am–10pm). The voice is functional first, friendly second: it tells you exactly what to do ("Watch the lights. Don't back up."), then gets out of the way. Personality lives in the small moments — the "Keep it clean." tagline, the wink of "Sit back, or do it yourself" — not in adjective-heavy sales copy.

---

## Voice Dimensions

### Formal ←—————→ Casual: **8/10** (Casual)

The brand uses contractions throughout ("Don't enter unless the light is green", "It's"), addresses readers in second person ("your car", "you want to do it yourself"), and writes in short conversational fragments ("Two locations. Heated indoor bays at Roosevelt Rd for winter."). Sentence rhythm is closer to spoken speech than written marketing.

**Evidence:**
- "Pull up & watch the lights."
- "Drive in, drive out — clean in 4½ minutes."
- "Drop a buck and clean out the cabin while your wax cures."
- "Wash it your way."

### Serious ←—————→ Playful: **6/10** (Lightly Playful)

Mostly utilitarian, but with consistent small bursts of wit. The tagline "Keep it clean." is a deliberate double meaning. Section headers personify the experience ("Sit back, or do it yourself", "Friendly faces, ready to help"). Exclamation marks and emoji are absent — playfulness shows up in word choice and rhythm, not punctuation.

**Evidence:**
- "Sit back, or **do it yourself**."
- "Four ways to make your car **shine**."
- Tagline pun: "Keep it clean."
- Restraint: zero exclamation marks across the entire site.

### Technical ←—————→ Simple: **7/10** (Mostly Simple)

Industry terms (LustraShield, spot-free rinse, presoak) are used freely but always grounded in plain explanation ("Let presoak dwell 10–20 seconds before rinsing"). The dial walkthrough teaches without condescending. Numbers replace jargon: "$4.00 for 5 mins" beats "competitively priced."

**Evidence:**
- "A surface gloss enhancer that beads water and leaves a deep shine."
- "No brushes. No swirl marks. No risk to fresh paint or ceramic coatings."
- FAQ explains touchless mechanics in one paragraph without an engineering tone.

### Reserved ←—————→ Bold: **6/10** (Quietly Confident)

Claims are direct but rarely hyperbolic. "The only place that does it right" appears in a testimonial, not in first-party copy. The brand prefers to let specifics carry the weight ("For 30 years.", "Heated indoor bays", "Both locations") rather than superlatives. Competitors are never named.

**Evidence:**
- "Forest Park's touchless car wash. For 30 years."
- "Each token = one Ultimate wash. Never expires."
- No "best", "#1", or "premier" anywhere in copy.

## Brand Personality

- **Primary Archetype: The Guide** — instructional, patient, methodical. Most page copy walks the customer step by step ("Pay at the station → Wait for green → Pull in & park → Watch the timer"). The brand's job is to remove friction and reduce uncertainty.
- **Secondary Archetype: The Friend** — neighborhood-warm, approachable, unpretentious. "Friendly faces, ready to help"; testimonials attributed as "Forest Park regular" and "Detail-obsessed neighbor."
- **Archetype Fit: Strong.** The Guide voice is consistent across the homepage flow, the self-serve dial walkthrough, and the FAQ. The Friend dimension shows up in testimonials, attendant copy, and the tagline.

The brand is **not** an Innovator (no future-of-car-wash language), not a Rebel (no anti-competitor jabs), and not an Authority (no awards, certifications, or expert credentialing).

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use

**Action verbs:** pull, drive, wait, watch, dial, tap, finish, sit back, drop, drive in, drive out, work (top-down), keep

**Descriptive words:** touchless, brushless, scratchless, trackless, heated, spot-free, hands-free, clean, glossy

**Value words:** spotless, clean, ready, simple, friendly, every day, never expires

**Locale words:** Forest Park, Roosevelt Rd, Madison St, neighborhood, Oak Park, Eisenhower

**Spec words (high frequency):** 7am–10pm, 4½ minutes, $4 / 5 mins, since the '90s, 30 years, two locations, nine settings

### Words We Avoid

- "Premier", "luxury", "elite", "ultimate" (used only as a tier name, never in prose)
- "World-class", "industry-leading", "best-in-class", "#1"
- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-generation"
- "Solutions" (as a noun for services)
- "Customer" — the brand says "you" and "your car"
- Competitor names (Crystal Car Wash, Mister, Tommy's) — never mentioned
- Aggressive sales pressure ("limited time", "act now", "while supplies last")
- Hashtags, emoji, exclamation marks

### Signature Phrases

- **Tagline:** "Keep it clean." (used in hero, location pages, footer kicker)
- **Positioning line:** "Forest Park's touchless car wash. For 30 years."
- **Recurring fragments:** "Drive in, drive out." · "Two locations." · "Open 7am–10pm, every day." · "Watch the lights." · "Wash it your way."
- **Linguistic patterns:**
  - Three-beat sentences ("No brushes. No swirl marks. No risk to fresh paint.")
  - Em-dashes for casual asides ("— Apple Pay too", "— never LustraShield as the last step")
  - Sentence fragments used for rhythm and emphasis ("Two locations. Heated indoor bays.")
  - Section eyebrows in numbered code-style: "01 / Wash packages"

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's

- **Lead with a concrete number or specific fact.** "Open 7am–10pm" beats "convenient hours." "4½ minutes" beats "fast."
- **Use contractions everywhere.** "Don't", "we're", "it's", "you'll." Anything else sounds stiff.
- **Address the reader as "you" and the car as "your car."** Never "customers" or "vehicles."
- **Write in three-beat fragments for emphasis.** "No brushes. No swirl marks. No risk." This is a signature pattern.
- **Anchor every benefit to Forest Park, Roosevelt Rd, or Madison St** when it's geographically relevant. Local proof is the moat.
- **Use em-dashes for casual asides.** "Tap, card, cash, or token — Apple Pay too."
- **Name the proprietary product.** LustraShield, spot-free rinse, air cannon dryers — these are brand assets.
- **Bold one or two words per heading for visual rhythm.** "Four ways to make your car **shine**."
- **Lead instructional copy with a verb.** "Pull in." "Watch the lights." "Tap to pay."
- **Mention the 30-year heritage when there's room.** It's the strongest single proof point in the brand.

### Don'ts

- **Don't use exclamation marks.** Zero. The brand's confidence comes from rhythm, not punctuation.
- **Don't use emoji in body copy.** A "✓" in a confirmation state is the maximum.
- **Don't use superlatives** ("best", "premier", "world-class", "#1"). Specifics carry the weight.
- **Don't name competitors.** The brand doesn't punch sideways.
- **Don't use urgency tactics.** No "limited time", "act fast", "today only." Out of voice.
- **Don't write paragraphs longer than three sentences.** Break it up.
- **Don't open with "We."** Open with the customer benefit, the verb, or the spec.
- **Don't write "solutions", "experience" (as a noun), or "journey."** Industry filler words.
- **Don't oversell the touchless angle without pairing it with self-serve.** The combo is the differentiator, not either alone.
- **Don't use formal customer-service language** ("We apologize for the inconvenience", "Dear valued customer"). Sounds corporate.

## Brand Messaging Hierarchy

### Tagline (3 words)
> **Keep it clean.**

Doubles as a wash promise and a behavioral nudge ("keep our lot clean"). Already in heavy use — preserve it.

### Value Propositions (1 sentence each)

1. **Touchless that protects your paint.** No brushes, no swirl marks, no risk to fresh paint or ceramic coatings — clean in 4½ minutes.
2. **Heated indoor bays at Roosevelt Rd.** The only car wash in Forest Park you can use at -10° without your locks freezing on the way out.
3. **Self-serve when you want control.** Nine pro settings — engine cleaner to LustraShield to spot-free rinse — for $4 every five minutes.
4. **Two locations, 7am–10pm, every day.** Roosevelt Rd and Madison St, open 105 hours a week, since the '90s.
5. **Tokens save you up to 20%.** Each one is an Ultimate wash, never expires, works at both locations.

### Elevator Pitch (≈75 words)

> Spotless Carwash has been Forest Park's touchless car wash for 30 years. Two locations on Roosevelt Rd and Madison St, open 7am to 10pm every day. Touchless automatic bays clean your car in four and a half minutes — no brushes, no scratches, heated indoor bays at Roosevelt for winter. Self-serve wand bays when you want to do it yourself: nine settings for four bucks. Tokens save you up to twenty percent and never expire.

### Boilerplate (≈130 words)

> Spotless Carwash is a family-operated, two-location car wash in Forest Park, Illinois, serving Forest Park, Oak Park, River Forest, and Eisenhower-corridor commuters since the 1990s. Both sites — 7343 Roosevelt Road and 7802 Madison Street — combine touchless automatic bays with self-serve wand bays, giving drivers a choice between hands-free convenience and hands-on control. The Roosevelt Road location features heated, enclosed automatic bays for year-round winter washing in Chicago weather. Wash packages run $8 to $12 with prepaid token bundles that save up to 20%, and the self-serve bays offer nine professional settings — including LustraShield surface gloss and spot-free rinse — for $4 per five minutes. Open every day, 7am to 10pm.

### Full Brand Story (300–500 words)

**Status: Missing.** The site references "since the '90s" and "30 years" but has no narrative About page, founder story, or origin paragraph. **Recommendation:** add a 300–400 word founder/origin story to the homepage or a dedicated About page covering: who started it, why Forest Park, why touchless before it was mainstream, what's stayed the same and what's changed across 30 years. This is the single highest-leverage piece of brand content currently absent.

## Competitor Voice Comparison

Based on the existing COMPETITOR-REPORT.md analysis. Crystal Car Wash is the primary direct competitor (1.5 mi away).

| Dimension | Spotless | Crystal Car Wash | Mister Car Wash (national reference) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | 8/10 | 6/10 | 7/10 |
| Serious ↔ Playful | 6/10 | 4/10 | 7/10 |
| Technical ↔ Simple | 7/10 | 8/10 | 8/10 |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | 6/10 | 7/10 | 8/10 |
| Primary Archetype | The Guide | The Authority | The Friend |

**Differentiation Assessment:**

- **Voice overlap with Crystal:** both use functional, benefit-driven copy. Crystal leans harder on membership-driven urgency ("Unlimited wash club") and big-tier names ("Platinum", "Gold").
- **Spotless's distinct voice territory:** patient, instructional, locally specific. Crystal does not teach you how to use their wash — they assume drive-through familiarity. Spotless's how-it-works walkthrough and self-serve dial guide are voice assets Crystal cannot replicate without a self-serve offering.
- **Unoccupied territory in the local market:** "30-year Forest Park heritage" is unclaimed. No competitor leans into longevity or local roots.
- **Recommendation:** double down on the Guide + neighbor combo. Add the founder/origin story. Use first names + neighborhoods in testimonials. Avoid drifting toward the membership-aggressive Crystal voice when a membership program eventually launches.

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

1. **Standardize the heritage reference.** Pick one — "Since 1994" (if accurate) or "For 30 years" — and use it consistently in hero, footer, and meta descriptions. "Since the '90s" is the weakest of the three and should be retired.
2. **Rewrite the email signup CTA in voice.** "Subscribe" → "Get the token sale alerts" or "Send me the deals." Pair it with a value exchange ("$5 off your next wash").
3. **Replace generic testimonial attributions** with first name + neighborhood ("Marcus, River Forest" instead of "Forest Park regular"). The current format reads as fabricated.
4. **De-duplicate the hero/bays "nothing touches" copy.** Pick one location for the touchless promise; vary the other.
5. **Add a Voice Chart card** to your team's internal Notion/Linear/wherever copy is briefed — the "IS / IS NOT" table above is the fastest reference for new freelancers or contractors.

### Voice Evolution Opportunities

1. **Write the founder/origin story** (300–400 words) and add an About page. This is the single biggest brand asset currently missing — 30 years of operation with no narrative is a wasted moat.
2. **Develop a Guide-archetype content series.** "How to wash your car in Chicago winter", "Touchless vs. brush wash: what the paint actually sees", "Why we don't have a membership (yet)" — all on-brand for the Guide voice and good for SEO.
3. **Create a microcopy library** for error states, confirmation messages, and transactional emails. The brand has no defined voice for these moments yet.
4. **Build a seasonal voice playbook** — winter (heated bays, salt removal), spring (pollen, post-winter detail), summer (interior care), fall (leaf sap). The voice stays the same; the topical hooks rotate.

### Consistency Improvements

1. **Document the signature patterns** (em-dashes, three-beat fragments, no exclamation marks) in a one-page style sheet. New writers won't know these are intentional unless told.
2. **Audit every CTA across the site** — most are on-brand ("See wash packages", "Buy tokens"), but any future "Sign up", "Learn more", or "Submit" buttons would break the pattern.
3. **Define social media voice rules.** The website has no social copy yet — when it launches, the temptation to use emoji and exclamation marks will be strong. Decide now whether social gets a slightly looser dialect or matches the site exactly.
4. **Lock the brand vocabulary list** (LustraShield, spot-free rinse, air cannon dryer, touchless automatic, self-serve wand) so capitalization, hyphenation, and product naming stays uniform across all future copy.
